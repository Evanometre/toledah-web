What Is Net Profit — And How Not To Calculate Your Business Net Profit Margins

March 13, 2026 · The Dispatch

Net profit is the amount of money a business keeps after all expenses have been deducted from revenue. It’s a fundamental metric to understand whether your business is actually making money, not just generating sales.

The Basics

Revenue: Total money your business brings in from sales or services.
Expenses: All costs incurred to run the business — including salaries, rent, utilities, raw materials, marketing, and taxes.
Net Profit: Revenue minus Expenses.

Example:
Revenue: ₦1,000,000
Expenses: ₦700,000
Net Profit: ₦300,000

How NOT To Calculate Net Profit Margins

Common mistakes founders make:

  • Ignoring hidden costs like utilities, subscriptions, or software amortization.
  • Using revenue only, instead of subtracting all operating expenses.
  • Mixing personal and business expenses.

Correct approach:

Net Profit Margin (%) = (Net Profit / Total Revenue) × 100
Example: (300,000 / 1,000,000) × 100 = 30%

This means for every ₦1 your business earns, ₦0.30 is actual profit after expenses.

Bottom Line

Net profit shows the real health of your business. Always subtract all expenses before calculating margins — skipping this step is one of the fastest ways to misjudge your company’s performance.
