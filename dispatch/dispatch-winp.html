Understanding Net Profit: How to Calculate Your Business Net Profit Margin, and what a healthy net profit margin is.

What is Net Profit?

Three terms that always show up in the business world are Revenue, Gross Profit, and Net Profit.

Revenue is simply the sum of all sales made by a business.

Gross Profit is the revenue less the cost of goods sold.

For example, if a business made a total of €5,400 in a week, and the total cost of products sold during that period was €4,800, the gross profit is:

€5,400 - €4,800 = €600

The Net Profit is the gross profit minus all expenses incurred to make those sales. Continuing with the example, if the business spent €100 on labour, €55 on energy, and €45 on servers, the net profit is:

€600 - (€100 + €55 + €45) = €400

The Net Profit Margin is calculated as:

Net Profit / Revenue × 100

Using our example: (€400 / €5,400) × 100 ≈ 7.41% approximately.

There are several tools that help business owners calculate this. Popular offline tools include Excel, and many online calculators exist. A more automatic method is using a POS system, which records sales and calculates net profit automatically. What is a Healthy Net Profit Margin?

The definition of a healthy net profit margin depends heavily on industry, business model, and location. A businessman comparing their pharmacy to a SaaS company would yield misleading results.

Industry / Business Type Typical Net Profit Margin
Large e-retail chains (Amazon, Walmart) 2–6%
Commodity distribution businesses 1–3%
Software / SaaS businesses 20–40%
💡 Tip: Track your net profit margin monthly. Watching trends over time is more valuable than one-off calculations.

Key Takeaways

  • Revenue – total sales.
  • Gross Profit – revenue minus cost of goods sold.
  • Net Profit – gross profit minus all operating expenses.
  • Net Profit Margin – net profit as a percentage of revenue.
  • Healthy margins vary by industry; low-margin operations are not necessarily failing.
